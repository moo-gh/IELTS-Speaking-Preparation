#### 1. Do you like the seaside?
**Yes**—**open** **horizons**, **salt** **air**, and the **sound** of **waves** **reset** my **head** **faster** than **most** **city** **parks**. **Crowded** **beaches** in **peak** **season** **stress** me **out**, but **quieter** **stretches** **still** **feel** **worth** the **trip**.

---
#### 2. How often do you go to the seaside?
**A** **few** **times** a **year**, **usually** on **long** **weekends** or when **relatives** **plan** **trips** **north** or **south** to the **coast**. **Daily** **life** keeps me **inland** **mostly**; I **treat** **sea** **visits** as **proper** **breaks**, not **routine**.

---
#### 3. What do you usually do at the seaside?
**Walk** along the **shore**, **watch** **sunset**, **eat** **grilled** **fish** or **snacks** from **vendors**, and **avoid** **strong** **midday** **sun** like my **parents** **taught** me. **Swimming** **depends** on **water** **quality** and **whether** I **brought** **proper** **shoes** for **rocky** **areas**.

---
#### 4. Did you go to the seaside when you were young?
**Yes**—**family** **holidays** with **packed** **cars**, **sand** in **every** **corner** of the **car**, and **cousins** **building** **dams** in **wet** **sand**. I **remember** **sunburn** **lectures** **more** **vividly** than **any** **single** **beach** **name**.

---
#### 5. Would you like to live near the sea?
**Part** of me **would**—**fresh** **breeze** and **walking** **routes** **sound** **appealing**. **Humidity**, **salt** **corrosion** on **cars**, and **storm** **seasons** **give** me **pause**; **visiting** **often** **might** **beat** **living** **full**-**time** **for** me.

---
#### 6. Is the seaside popular in your country?
**Very**—**northern** and **southern** **coasts** **fill** with **domestic** **tourists** in **summer**, and **even** **winter** **trips** **draw** **people** to **milder** **southern** **cities**. **Resorts**, **fishing** **towns**, and **historic** **ports** **all** **compete** for **weekend** **crowds**.
