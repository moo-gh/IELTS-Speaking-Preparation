#### 1. Do you enjoy celebrating birthdays?
**Generally**, yes—it's a **low-pressure** **excuse** to **catch up** with people and **eat** something **nice**, even if I'm not **big on** **huge** **parties**. I **don't** **need** **fanfare** every year, but I **appreciate** **thoughtful** **messages** and **quality** time more than **over-the-top** **events**.

---
#### 2. How do people usually celebrate birthdays in your country?
**Quite** **varied**—some families **mark** it with a **homemade** **cake** and **dinner** at home, while others **book** a **restaurant** or **hire** a **venue** for **friends**. **Kids'** parties **often** **revolve** around **games** and **goodie** **bags**, whereas **adults** might **keep** it **low-key** with **drinks** or a **weekend** **trip**.

---
#### 3. What birthday is considered especially important where you live?
**Eighteen** **tends to** **stand out** because it's **tied** to **legal** **milestones** like **voting**—people might **throw** a **bigger** **gathering** or **receive** a **meaningful** **gift** from **parents**. **Round-number** birthdays—**thirty**, **forty**—also get **extra** **attention**, almost like **mini** **life** **reviews**.

---
#### 4. What did you do on your last birthday?
I **kept** it **fairly** **simple**: **dinner** with **close** **friends**, then **coffee** and **cake** **afterwards**. Work was **hectic** that week, so I **wasn't** **up for** a **late** **night**, but it still felt **special** because people **made** an **effort** to **show up**—**that's** what **counts** for me.

---
#### 5. Do you prefer to celebrate your birthday with family or with friends?
**It depends on the year**—sometimes I **crave** the **comfort** of **family** **traditions** and **home-cooked** food; other times I **want** **laughs** and **inside** **jokes** with **friends** I've **chosen**. If I **had** to **pick** one **default**, I'd **lean** towards **friends** in **recent** years because my **schedule** **aligns** **better** with theirs.

---
#### 6. Are birthdays more important for children or for adults?
For **children**, birthdays are **huge**—they're **counting down** **weeks** **ahead** and **remember** the **details** for **months**. **Adults** **often** **downplay** them, but **milestones** can still **hit** **hard** emotionally, especially if someone's **reflecting** on **goals** or **relationships**. **Importance** **shifts** from **excitement** to **meaning**.

---
#### 7. What kinds of birthday gifts are common in your culture?
**Practical** items—**gadgets**, **books**, **skincare**—are **popular** because people **want** gifts to **get used**. **Experiences** like **concert** **tickets** or **spa** **vouchers** are **increasingly** common too, especially among **people** who **already** **have** **enough** **stuff**. **Cash** in a **card** still **happens**, though it can feel **impersonal** unless it's **clearly** **what** the **recipient** **needs**.
