#### 1. Do you like insects?
**From** **a** **distance**, **yes**—**bees** on **flowers** and **butterflies** in **parks** are **fascinating** to **watch**. **Up** **close** on my **skin** or in my **kitchen**, I'm **less** **enthusiastic**; **respect** **doesn't** **mean** I **want** them **roommates**.

---
#### 2. What insects are common where you live?
**Ants**, **flies**, **mosquitoes** in **warm** **months**, **spiders** in **corners**, and **occasional** **beetles** **near** **lights** at **night**. **Gardens** **attract** **bees** and **ladybirds**; **pigeon** **zones** **aren't** **insects**, but **wasps** **sometimes** **hover** around **bins**.

---
#### 3. Are there many insects near your home?
**Seasonal** **peaks**—**summer** **balconies** **collect** **mosquitoes** after **rain**, and **spring** **brings** **more** **flying** **insects** **around** **plants**. **Winter** **quiets** **things** **down** **indoors**, though **ants** **still** **find** **crumbs** if I'm **careless**.

---
#### 4. Do insects bother you?
**Mosquito** **bites** and **buzzing** **near** my **ear** at **night** **definitely** do. **Harmless** **crawlies** I **relocate** with **paper**; **stinging** **ones** I **avoid** **rather** than **heroically** **battle**. **Hygiene** and **screens** **prevent** **most** **dramas**.

---
#### 5. Have you ever seen any interesting insects?
**Once** a **huge** **mantis** on a **hiking** **trail**—**slow**, **alien**-**looking**, **perfectly** **still** until it **snapped** at a **fly**. **School** **science** **classes** with **microscopes** **also** **revealed** **details** I'd **never** **noticed** with **bare** **eyes**.

---
#### 6. Would you like to learn more about insects?
**A** **little**—enough to **know** which **local** **species** **help** **pollination** versus **which** **carry** **disease** or **damage** **crops**. I **won't** **become** an **entomologist**, but **basic** **knowledge** **beats** **panicking** at **every** **six**-**legged** **visitor**.
