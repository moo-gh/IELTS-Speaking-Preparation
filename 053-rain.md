#### 1. Do you like rainy weather?
**It** **depends** **on** **the** **dose**—a **light** **drizzle** after **heat** can feel **refreshing**, and the **sound** on a **window** is **soothing** when I'm **reading** **indoors**. **Nonstop** **downpours** that **flood** **pavements** and **delay** **buses** **wear** me **down** **fast** **though**; I **respect** rain **more** than I **romanticise** it.

---
#### 2. Does it rain much where you live?
**Seasonally**, **yes**—we get **stretches** of **dry** **weather**, then **weeks** when **grey** **clouds** **hang** **around** and **umbrellas** never **quite** **dry** **out**. **Totals** are **moderate** **compared** with **tropical** **places**, but **winter** **showers** can **feel** **persistent** because **daylight** is **already** **short**.

---
#### 3. What do you usually do on rainy days?
If I'm **free**, I **treat** it as **permission** to **slow** **down**—**tea**, a **podcast**, or **tidying** **small** **corners** of the **flat** I'd **skip** on **sunny** **afternoons**. If I **have** **commutes** and **errands**, I'm **pragmatic**: **waterproof** **jacket**, **sensible** **shoes**, and I **accept** I'll **arrive** **looking** **slightly** **rumpled**.

---
#### 4. Have you ever been caught in the rain without an umbrella?
**Plenty** of **times**—**forecast** **lies**, **meetings** run **over**, or I **leave** the **brolly** on the **train** **by** **mistake**. The **worst** is **sudden** **summer** **bursts** when you're **dressed** for **sun**; you **seek** **shelter** under an **awning**, **laugh** it **off** with **strangers**, then **drip** onto the **office** **floor**.

---
#### 5. How does rain affect daily life in your city?
It **slows** **traffic**, **backs** **up** **junctions**, and **makes** **cycling** **sketchy** when **paint**-**marked** **lanes** get **slick**. **Outdoor** **markets** **pack** **up** **early**, **cafés** **fill** **up**, and **basements** in **older** **streets** **sometimes** **struggle** after **heavy** **storms**. **Farmers** and **gardeners** **welcome** it **more** **than** **commuters** do, **honestly**.

---
#### 6. Would you like to live in a wetter or drier place?
**Marginally** **drier**, **if** I **had** to **choose**—I'd **trade** a **few** **gloom**-**heavy** **weeks** for **cleaner** **windows** on **plans** and **fewer** **muddy** **shoe** **trails** **across** the **hall**. I wouldn't **want** **desert** **conditions** **either**; **some** **rain** keeps **everything** **greener** and the **air** **less** **dusty**.
