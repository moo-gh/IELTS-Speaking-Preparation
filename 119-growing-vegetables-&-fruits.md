#### 1. Are you interested in growing vegetables and fruits?
**Moderately**, **yes**—I **like** the **idea** of **fresh** **herbs** and **tomatoes** on a **balcony**, even if I'm **not** a **full**-**time** **farmer**. **Watching** **something** **grow** **feels** **rewarding** when **study** **stress** **piles** **up**.

---
#### 2. Is growing vegetables popular in your country?
**In** **villages**, **very**—**families** **maintain** **plots** and **share** **harvests** **seasonally**. **City** **people** **grow** **less**, though **balcony** **pots** and **community** **gardens** **spread** among **younger** **residents** **recently**.

---
#### 3. Do you think it is easy to grow vegetables?
**Depends** on **what** and **where**—**herbs** and **lettuce** **tolerate** **beginners**; **tomatoes** **need** **sun** and **patience**; **pests** and **irregular** **watering** **kill** **enthusiasm** **fast**. **Good** **soil** and **consistent** **care** **matter** **more** than **luck**.

---
#### 4. Should schools teach students how to grow vegetables?
**Yes**, **at** **least** **basics**—**patience**, **responsibility**, and **respect** for **food** **sources** **beat** **pure** **theory**. **Short** **garden** **projects** **also** **give** **outdoor** **breaks** from **screens** and **desks**.

---
#### 5. Have you ever grown anything yourself?
**A** **few** **pots**—**mint**, **basil**, and **once** **cherry** **tomatoes** that **birds** **attacked** before I **harvested** **much**. **My** **grandmother** **grew** **more** **seriously**; I **mostly** **helped** **water** and **weed** as a **child**.

---
#### 6. Do you prefer home-grown or shop-bought produce?
**Home**-**grown** when **quality** is **good**—**taste** and **trust** **win**—but **shops** **save** **time** and **offer** **variety** **year**-**round**. **Realistically**, I **buy** **most** **produce** and **grow** **small** **extras** for **fun**.
