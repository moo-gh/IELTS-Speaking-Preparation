#### 1. What public holidays do you have in your country?
**Several** **through** the **year**—**religious** **dates** like **Eid** **periods**, **national** **days**, and **Nowruz** **marking** **spring** with **family** **visits** and **spring** **cleaning**. **Some** **offices** **close** for **long** **stretches** around **major** **festivals**, while **others** **only** **pause** for **one** **official** **day**.

---
#### 2. Do you like public holidays?
**Yes**, **mostly**—they **break** the **routine** and **justify** **rest** **without** **guilt**. The **downside** is **crowded** **roads** and **sold-out** **trains** when **everyone** **travels** **at** **once**; **peace** **depends** on **whether** I **stay** **local** or **visit** **relatives**.

---
#### 3. What do you usually do on public holidays?
**Family** **meals**, **short** **trips** to **see** **cousins**, or **quiet** **days** at **home** if I've **been** **burning** the **candle** at **both** **ends**. I **rarely** **shop** on **busy** **streets**; I **prefer** **walks**, **films**, or **catching** up on **sleep** and **laundry**.

---
#### 4. Which public holiday is your favourite?
**Nowruz**, **probably**—the **table** **setting**, **fresh** **greens**, and the **sense** of a **clean** **slate** **feel** **hopeful** even if **weather** is **still** **chilly**. **Food** is **lighter** than **winter** **feasts**, and **people** **forgive** **small** **arguments** **more** **easily**, **it** **seems**.

---
#### 5. Do you think public holidays are important?
**They** **are**—they **anchor** **culture**, **let** **workers** **recover**, and **give** **families** **shared** **dates** to **meet** **without** **negotiating** **calendars** **forever**. **Too** **many** **unpaid** **shutdowns** can **hurt** **small** **businesses**, so **balance** **matters**, but **rest** **isn't** **luxury**.

---
#### 6. How do public holidays affect people's daily life?
**Shops** **change** **hours**, **traffic** **peaks** then **empties**, and **students** **vanish** from **libraries** while **parks** **fill**. **Some** **people** **work** **overtime** in **hospitality** or **transport**; **others** **finally** **switch** off **email**—**the** **same** **day** **feels** **different** depending on **your** **job**.
