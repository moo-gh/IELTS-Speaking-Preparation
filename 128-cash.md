#### 1. Do you often carry cash with you?
**Some**, **not** **much**—**small** **notes** for **taxis**, **street** **food**, or **shops** that **refuse** **cards**. **Most** **daily** **spending** **runs** on **phone** **payments**, so **wallet** **cash** **stays** **minimal**.

---
#### 2. How often do you use cash?
**A** **few** **times** a **month**, **mainly** when **digital** **systems** **fail** or **vendors** **prefer** **notes**. **Bills** and **formal** **receipts** **usually** **go** through **bank** **apps** **now**.

---
#### 3. Do you prefer paying with cash or cards?
**Cards** and **apps** for **tracking** **spending** and **avoiding** **lost** **bills** in **pockets**. **Cash** **feels** **tangible** for **tiny** **purchases**, but **counting** **change** in **queues** **annoys** me **on** **busy** **days**.

---
#### 4. Is cash still popular in your country?
**Less** than **before**, **especially** in **cities**, but **markets**, **buses**, and **older** **shopkeepers** **still** **expect** **notes**. **Rural** **areas** and **family** **gatherings** **use** **cash** **more** **openly** for **gifts** and **tips**.

---
#### 5. Have you ever lost cash or had it stolen?
**Small** **amounts** **once**—**dropped** **change** from a ** torn** **pocket** and **left** a **note** in an **old** **jacket** **months** **later**. **Nothing** **dramatic**, but it **taught** me to **carry** **less** **and** **check** **zips**.

---
#### 6. Do you think society will become cashless?
**Mostly**, **over** **time**—**younger** **people** **barely** **carry** **wallets**. **Cash** will **likely** **remain** for **emergencies**, **privacy**, and **people** **without** **bank** **access**, so **complete** **disappearance** **seems** **unlikely** **soon**.
