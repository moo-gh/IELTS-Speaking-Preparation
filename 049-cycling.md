#### 1. Do you like cycling?
**Yes**—it **feels** **liberating** to **glide** along when the **road** is **smooth** and the **route** is **clear**. I **enjoy** it **less** in **driving** **rain**, on **ice**, or when **drivers** are **rushing** **past** **too** **tightly**, but on a **calm** **day** a **bike** is a **practical** way to **move** and **breathe** **fresh** **air** at the **same** **time**.

---
#### 2. How often do you go cycling?
**On** **average**, not **all** that **often**—maybe a **handful** of **rides** a **month** when I'm **visiting** **friends** or **trying** to **run** a **local** **errand** **without** a **car**. If I had **safer** **cycle** **lanes** **end-to-end** from **home** to **work**, I'd **use** a **bike** **far** **more** **consistently**.

---
#### 3. Is cycling popular in your country?
**Fairly**—**especially** in **bigger** **cities** where **bike-share** **schemes** and **dedicated** **paths** are **gradually** **expanding**. In **rural** **areas** people may **rely** on **motorbikes** or **cars** for **longer** **distances**, so **cycling** isn't **universal**, but you still see **families** **out** at **weekends** and **commuters** in **high-vis** **gear** on **quieter** **arteries**.

---
#### 4. When was the last time you rode a bicycle?
**A** **few** **weeks** **ago**—I **borrowed** a **friend's** **hybrid** and we **cycled** along a **river** **path** for an **afternoon** **outing**. Before that, I mostly **docked** a **public** **hire** **bike** for **short** **hops** in the **centre** when **traffic** was **lighter** and I wasn't **carrying** **heavy** **bags**.

---
#### 5. Did you have a bike when you were a child?
**Yes**—I **owned** a **sturdy** **second-hand** **mountain** **bike** with a **basket** on the **front** for my **water** **bottle** and I **treated** it like **freedom** after **school**. I **learned** to **check** the **tyres** and **tighten** the **brakes** **imperfectly**; **wobbling** at **first** was **part** of the **process**, but I **grew** **more** **confident** on **familiar** **streets** **near** **home**.

---
#### 6. Do you think cycling is a good way to get around in cities?
**In** **principle**, **yes**—bikes can **cut** **congestion** and **emissions** if **infrastructure** is **up** to **scratch**: **separate** **lanes**, **clear** **signage**, and **sensible** **speed** **limits** for **vehicles**. The **caveat** is **safety** and **convenience**; without **reliable** **parking** and **protection** at **junctions**, many **people** will **rationally** **choose** **buses** or **trains** **instead** **of** **risking** **a** **close** **call** with **traffic**.
