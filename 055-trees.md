#### 1. Do you like trees?
**Definitely**—they **quiet** **down** noisy **streets**, **shade** **pavements** in **summer**, and **remind** you that **concrete** isn't the **whole** **world**. I'm **not** a **botanist**, but **walking** past **mature** **oaks** or along **rows** of **street** **trees** after a **stressful** day **settles** my **thoughts** faster than **scrolling** on my **phone**.

---
#### 2. Are there many trees where you live?
**Urban** parts are a **mixed** **bag**: **historic** **parks** and some **boulevards** are **thick** with **canopy**, but **newer** **developments** can feel **thin** until **young** **saplings** **catch** **up**. **Inner** **courtyards** and **back** **lanes** sometimes **squeeze** in **pear** or **plum** **trees** planted by **past** **residents**, which **breaks** up the **grey** **geometry** **nicely**.

---
#### 3. Did you ever climb trees as a child?
**Once** **in** **a** **while** at **cousins**' **houses** or on **school** **trips**—**nothing** **daring**, **mostly** low **branches** I could **trust** while **wearing** scruffy **trainers**. I was **more** **cautious** than **adventurous**; I'd **rather** sit **under** **shade** and **watch** peers **risk** **skinned** **knees** than **gamble** **ankles** myself.

---
#### 4. What kinds of trees are common where you live?
**Plane** **trees** **crop** **up** beside **busy** **roads** because they **handle** **pollution** relatively **well**—**silver** **birches**, **horse** **chestnuts**, and **maples** **dot** **parks**. **Garden** **walls** hide **fruit** **trees** (**apple**, **fig**) in **sunnier** **plots**. I'm **hazy** on **labels** like **native** **versus** **introduced**, **but** **planted** varieties **still** shape how the **street** **feels**.

---
#### 5. Would you like to plant more trees in your neighbourhood?
**Yes**, **provided** they're **chosen** **sensibly**—**matching** **soil**, **avoiding** **roots** **rupturing** **pavements**, and **maintaining** them so they **don't** **become** **hazards**. **Grassroots** projects and **school** partnerships feel **motivating**; **trees** aren't **magic**, but they're one of the **few** **cheap** wins that **benefit** **wildlife**, **mental** **health**, and **summer** **temperatures** all **at** **once**.

---
#### 6. Have you ever been to a forest or large woodland?
**Yes**—**day** **trips** to **protected** **woodland** reserves and **misty** **hikes** **on** **trails** that **narrow** **under** **needle** cover. It's **dramatically** **quieter** than the **city**; you **notice** **moss**, **broken** **light**, and **mud** patterns as **stress** drops a **few** **notches**. I **wish** **I** visited **often** enough for it **to feel** **routine**.
