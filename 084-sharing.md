#### 1. Do you like sharing things with others?
**Yes**, **within** **reason**—**notes**, **food** at **gatherings**, and **honest** **advice** **feel** **natural** among **people** I **trust**. I **draw** **lines** around **privacy**, **expensive** **gear**, and **time** I **can't** **afford** to **give** **away**.

---
#### 2. What kind of things do you like to share?
**Study** **tips**, **extra** **snacks**, **podcast** **recommendations**, and **occasionally** **chargers** when **someone's** **phone** **dies**. **Emotional** **support** **matters** **too**—**listening** **without** **immediately** **fixing** **everything**.

---
#### 3. Did you share things with friends when you were young?
**Constantly**—**toys**, **sweets**, **comics**, and **bad** **ideas** like **dares** on **playgrounds**. **Teachers** **pushed** **sharing** in **class**; **parents** **reminded** us when **siblings** **fought** over **the** **same** **remote**.

---
#### 4. Is it good for children to share?
**Generally** **yes**—it **builds** **empathy**, **patience**, and **social** **skills** they'll **need** in **teams** **later**. **Forced** **sharing** of **special** **treasures** can **backfire**; **guidance** **works** **better** than **public** **shaming**.

---
#### 5. Do people in your country share food?
**Very** **much**—**guests** **expect** **offers**, **family** **tables** **revolve** around **generous** **portions**, and **neighbours** **swap** **dishes** at **festivals**. **Refusing** **politely** is an **art**; **accepting** **too** **little** can **look** **rude**.

---
#### 6. Would you share your belongings with strangers?
**Small** **acts** **maybe**—**directions**, **spare** **change** for **bus** **fare**, **water** on a **hot** **day**. **Valuables** or **personal** **documents**, **no**—**kindness** **shouldn't** **ignore** **basic** **safety** and **boundaries**.
