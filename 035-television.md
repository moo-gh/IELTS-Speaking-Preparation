#### 1. Do you often watch TV?
Not as much as I used to—**streaming** and short-form video have **eaten into** traditional TV time. I still **flick on** the news in the morning or catch a **documentary** on a **weeknight** if I'm too tired to **scroll** through apps. Live TV feels a bit **old-fashioned** compared to **on-demand**, but I **keep up** with **current affairs** through **bulletins** when I can.

---
#### 2. What kind of TV programmes do you like?
I'm **drawn to** well-made **true-crime** series and **in-depth** interviews—anything that makes me **think** rather than **zone out**. **Light-hearted** **quiz shows** are fun **background** viewing when I'm cooking. I **steer clear of** **reality TV** that feels **scripted** or **overly dramatic**; life is dramatic enough already.

---
#### 3. Do you prefer watching TV alone or with others?
**It varies**. For news or **gritty** dramas I often watch alone so I can **concentrate** and **rewind** if I miss a line. Comedies or **sporting events** are better **in company**—you get the **reaction** in the room and it's easier to **debate** what just happened. **Spoilers** are less of an issue when you're watching together **live**.

---
#### 4. Has the internet changed the way people watch TV where you live?
Completely—**linear** schedules don't **rule** people's evenings anymore. Most people I know **binge** whole seasons on **apps**, **pause** whenever they want, and **skip** ads if they can pay for **premium** tiers. **Traditional** broadcasters have had to **adapt** with **catch-up** services and their own platforms so they don't **lose viewers** to **global** competitors.

---
#### 5. What kinds of TV programmes are popular in your country?
**Soap operas** and **domestic** dramas still pull in **huge** audiences among **older** viewers, while **younger** people **lean towards** **foreign** series with **subtitles**, **gaming** streams, and **variety** shows on **social** platforms. **National** **sport**, especially football, **dominates** weekend **ratings** when there's a big match.

---
#### 6. Do you think children watch too much TV?
**On balance**, many kids probably **get more screen time** than is ideal—not only TV but tablets and phones. **Parental controls** and **clear limits** help, but it's **easier said than done** when parents are **busy** or **exhausted**. **Educational** programmes in **moderation** are fine; the problem is **passive** watching **replacing** sleep, **outdoor play**, or **face-to-face** chat.
