#### 1. Do you like looking at clouds?
**Yes**, **especially** on **slow** **walks** or from a **window** when I'm **stuck** **indoors** **studying**. **Shapes** **shift** **fast** enough to **feel** **calming** without **demanding** **my** **full** **attention**.

---
#### 2. What kinds of clouds do you notice?
**Flat** **grey** **sheets** before **rain**, **fluffy** **white** **puffs** on **clear** **spring** **days**, and **thin** **streaks** at **sunset** that **turn** **pink** or **orange**. I **don't** **name** **formal** **types** **fluently**, but I **recognise** **"rain** **coming"** versus **"nice** **day"** **pretty** **well**.

---
#### 3. Did you learn about clouds at school?
**Briefly** in **science**—**water** **cycle**, **condensation**, and **basic** **weather** **symbols** on **maps**. **Practical** **memory** came from **looking** **up** **before** **football** **matches** and **deciding** whether to **bring** a **jacket**.

---
#### 4. Do cloudy days affect your mood?
**A** **little**—**endless** **grey** **weeks** **feel** **heavy**, but **one** **overcast** **day** can be **cosy** with **tea** and **books**. **Bright** **sun** **lifts** me **more** **reliably**, though **clouds** **sometimes** **help** **focus** by **cutting** **glare** on **screens**.

---
#### 5. What is the sky like where you live?
**Often** **clear** and **dry**, with **dramatic** **sunsets** over **hills** or **city** **silhouettes**. **Winter** **smog** or **dust** **can** **mute** **colours** **occasionally**; **after** **rain** the **sky** **scrubs** **itself** **clean** and **looks** **sharp**.

---
#### 6. Do you take photos of the sky?
**Sometimes**—**sunsets**, **storm** **edges**, or **unusual** **light** after **hail** when **everything** **feels** **temporary** and **sharp**. My **camera** **roll** is **mostly** **screenshots** and **study** **notes**, but **sky** **shots** **survive** the **annual** **cleanup**.
