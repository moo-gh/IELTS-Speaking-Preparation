#### 1. Do you like tea or coffee?
**Coffee** gets me through **grim** **Monday** **morning** **starts**—I **sip** something **milky**, **nothing** **too** **bitter**. **Tea** wins on **cold** **evenings** and when my **throat** feels **scratchy**: a **proper** **mug**, **warming** hands on the **outside**, **paired** **with** a **plain** **biscuit**.

---
#### 2. Which one do people in your country prefer?
**Older** **relatives** and **traditional** **households** **lean** **heavily** **towards** **black** tea with optional **rock** sugar or **dried** **lime**—it's the **default** **hospitality** **drink** at **visits**. **Younger** **crowds** in **cafés** order **cold** **brews**, **specialty** espressos, and **flavoured** **lattes** more often, so tastes **vary** sharply **between** **age** **groups**, **honestly**.

#### 4. Do you often drink tea or coffee when you meet friends?

**Naturally**—I'd **offer** tea **first**, then **coffee** if they **look** tired or **explicitly** **prefer** it. **Guests** shouldn't **fight** thirst while **balancing** plates on their **knees**; I **stock** herbal **bags** **too**, because **sleep** schedules differ and **some** **friends** **skip** caffeine **altogether**.
