#### 1. Do you like cooking?
**Generally**, yes—it's **relaxing** when I'm not **starving** and **under** **pressure**. I enjoy **chopping** **vegetables** and **experimenting** with **spices**, but I'm less **keen** on **deep-cleaning** the **kitchen** afterwards, so I try to **wash up** as I go so it doesn't **pile up**.

---
#### 2. How often do you cook?
Most **weeknights** I **cook** something **simple** at home—**stir-fries**, **pasta**, **soup**—because it's **cheaper** and **healthier** than **takeaway** every day. **Weekends** are more **hit-and-miss**: **sometimes** I'll **batch-cook** **stews**, **other** times I'll **meet** **friends** and **eat out** instead.

---
#### 3. What kind of food can you cook well?
I'd say I'm **reliable** with **one-pot** **meals** and **rice-based** dishes—**nothing** **restaurant-level**, but **consistent** and **edible**. **Baking** is my **weak** **spot**; my **cakes** **tend to** come out **dense** or **lopsided**, so I **stick to** **savoury** **recipes** where **precision** matters less.

---
#### 4. Who taught you to cook?
**Mainly** my **mother**, **starting** with **basic** **knife** **skills** and **food** **safety**—**washing** **hands**, **not** **cross-contaminating** **raw** meat. **Later** I **picked up** ideas from **YouTube** **tutorials** and **cookbooks**, but the **foundation**—**tasting** as you go and **adjusting** **seasoning**—came from **watching** her **at** **home**.

---
#### 5. Do you prefer eating at home or eating out?
**It depends** on the **mood** and **schedule**. **Eating** **at** **home** is **more** **budget-friendly** and you **control** **portion** **sizes** and **ingredients**. **Eating** **out** is **nice** for **special** **occasions** or when I want **cuisine** I **wouldn't** **attempt** myself—**authentic** **sushi**, for example, is **beyond** my **skill** **set**.

---
#### 6. Do people in your country cook at home a lot?
**Many** **families** still **cook** **daily** **meals** **from** **scratch**, especially **older** **generations** who **value** **home-made** **bread** and **stews**. **Young** **professionals** in **cities** **often** **rely** on **delivery** **apps** during **busy** **weeks**, but **home** **cooking** hasn't **disappeared**—it's just **competing** with **convenience** and **long** **commutes**.
