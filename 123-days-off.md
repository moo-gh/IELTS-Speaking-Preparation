#### 1. Do you have many days off?
**Weekends**, **mainly**, plus **public** **holidays** and **occasional** **extra** **days** around **exams** or **family** **events**. **During** **busy** **terms**, **true** **rest** **feels** **rare** even when **classes** **pause**—**assignments** **follow** you **home**.

---
#### 2. What do you usually do on your days off?
**Sleep** **in**, **catch** **up** on **reading**, **meet** **friends**, **run** **errands**, and **sometimes** **take** **short** **trips** **outside** **town**. If I'm **exhausted**, I **keep** it **quiet** **indoors**; if **energy** **returns**, I **walk** or **explore** **new** **cafés**.

---
#### 3. Do you usually spend your days off with your parents or friends?
**A** **mix**—**family** **meals** and **visits** **one** **day**, **friends** **another**, and **solo** **time** when **everyone** **is** **busy**. **Balance** **shifts** with **exam** **seasons** when **social** **plans** **shrink** **fast**.

---
#### 4. Do you prefer to relax or be active on your days off?
**Relax** **first**, **active** **second**—I **recover** **sleep**, then **walk**, **play** **sport**, or **sightsee** if **weather** **helps**. **All**-**action** **weekends** **without** **rest** **leave** me **more** **tired** **Monday** **morning**.

---
#### 5. What would you like to do if you had more days off?
**Travel** **slowly**—**mountain** **towns**, **coastal** **cities**, or **long** **visits** to **relatives** **without** **checking** **email** **every** **hour**. **Short** **courses** in **photography** or **cooking** **also** **appeal** when **budget** **allows**.

---
#### 6. Are days off important?
**Essential**—**burnout** **hits** **hard** when **weeks** **blur** **together** **without** **breaks**. **Rest** **improves** **focus**, **mood**, and **relationships**; **treating** **days** **off** as **guilty** **luxury** **backfires** **eventually**.
