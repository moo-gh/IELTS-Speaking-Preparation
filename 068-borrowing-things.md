#### 1. Have you borrowed books from others?
**Yes**—**course** **textbooks** from **classmates** when **mine** **arrived** **late**, and **novels** from **friends** who **insisted** I'd **like** a **series**. I **return** them **promptly** with **no** **coffee** **rings**; **damaged** **spines** **stress** me **out** as a **borrower**.

---
#### 2. Have you ever borrowed money from others?
**Small** **amounts** as a **student**—**bus** **fare** or **splitting** a **bill** when **cards** **failed**—**always** **paid** **back** the **same** **day**. I **avoid** **large** **loans** between **friends** because **money** can **poison** **trust** if **repayment** **drags**.

---
#### 3. Do you like to lend things to others?
**If** I **trust** the **person** and the **item** is **replaceable**—**chargers**, **umbrellas**, **extra** **notes**. I'm **hesitant** with **laptops**, **heirlooms**, or **anything** I **need** **tomorrow** for **work**; **one** **missed** **deadline** **teaches** you **boundaries**.

---
#### 4. How do you feel when people don't return things they borrowed from you?

---
#### 5. What things are not suitable to be borrowed?
**Toothbrushes**, **headphones** **in** **ears**, **prescription** **medication**, and **intimate** **clothing**—**obvious** **hygiene** **lines**. I'd **also** **avoid** **lending** **passports**, **IDs**, or **signed** **contracts**; the **risk** **outweighs** **any** **favour**.

---

**Libraries** for **volume** and **deadlines** that **force** you to **finish**; **friends** for **books** **libraries** **don't** **stock** or **quick** **notes** before an **exam**. **Friends** **feel** **warmer**, but **libraries** **spare** **friendships** when **life** gets **busy** and **returns** **slip**.
