#### 1. Do you like to keep things tidy?
**Yes**, **within** **reason**—**clear** **desks** and **sorted** **notes** **help** me **focus**, but I **don't** **chase** **perfection** **every** **hour**. **A** **little** **clutter** **during** **busy** **weeks** **feels** **normal**; **total** **mess** **stresses** me **out** **fast**.

---
#### 2. Did you use to keep your room tidy as a child?
**Not** **really**—**toys**, **books**, and **clothes** **piled** **up** until **parents** **nagged** me before **guests** **arrived**. **School** **teachers** **praised** **neat** **exercise** **books**, but my **bedroom** **looked** **more** like a **small** **warehouse** **explosion**.

---
#### 3. How do you keep your work or study space tidy?
**Weekly** **reset**—**bin** **scrap** **paper**, **file** **handouts**, **charge** **devices** in **one** **corner**, and **wipe** the **desk** **surface**. **During** **exam** **seasons**, I **use** **folders** by **subject** so **panic** **doesn't** **spread** **papers** **everywhere**.

---
#### 4. Do you think that it is necessary to be tidy?
**Useful**, **not** **mandatory** for **everyone**—**tidy** **spaces** **save** **time** and **reduce** **stress** for **study** and **work**. **Creative** **people** **sometimes** **thrive** in **controlled** **chaos**, but **shared** **homes** **need** **basic** **rules** so **others** **aren't** **annoyed**.

---
#### 5. What do you do when your space gets messy?
**Stop** and **spend** **twenty** **minutes** **sorting** **before** it **snowballs**—**music** on, **trash** **out**, **dishes** to **kitchen**, **books** **back** on **shelves**. If I'm **too** **tired**, I **at** **least** **clear** the **desk** **surface** so **morning** **study** **isn't** **blocked**.

---
#### 6. Do your family members keep things tidy?
**Mixed**—**my** **mother** **likes** **order** in **kitchens** and **living** **rooms**; **siblings** **leave** **trails** of **cups** and **chargers** **behind** them. **We** **compromise** with **shared** **clean**-**up** **days** rather than **arguing** **daily** over **every** **sock**.
