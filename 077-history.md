#### 1. Do you like history?
**Yes**, **in** **small** **doses**—**stories** about **how** **cities** **grew**, **wars** **ended**, or **everyday** **life** **changed** **fascinate** me **more** than **memorising** **long** **lists** of **dates**. **Documentaries** and **museum** **visits** **stick** **better** than **dry** **textbook** **pages**.

---
#### 2. Did you study history at school?
**We** **did**—**national** **history**, **world** **events**, and **basic** **timelines** from **primary** through **secondary** **school**. I **remember** **maps** and **projects** **more** than **exams**; **group** **presentations** on **ancient** **civilisations** were **surprisingly** **fun**.

**Probably** the **early** **twentieth** **century**—**rapid** **technology**, **political** **upheaval**, and **ordinary** **people** **adjusting** to **cars**, **radios**, and **wars**. **Local** **history** **interests** me **too**: **how** **streets** and **markets** **looked** **before** **modern** **blocks** **arrived**.

**Yes**—**old** **bazaars**, **forts**, **houses** **turned** into **museums**, and **ruins** on **day** **trips**. I **prefer** **guided** **context** or **good** **captions**; **empty** **stones** **without** **explanation** **leave** me **cold** **quickly**.

**Very**—it **explains** **why** **borders**, **customs**, and **conflicts** **exist** **today**, and it **warns** against **repeating** **obvious** **mistakes**. **Ignoring** **history** **doesn't** make **problems** **vanish**; it **just** makes **debates** **shallower**.

---
