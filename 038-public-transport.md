#### 1. How often do you use public transport?
**Fairly regularly** during the week—I **commute** by **metro** or **bus** when I don't **cycle**, especially if the weather's **awful** or I'm **running late**. At weekends I use it less; I'll **hop on** a tram **downtown** if parking would be a **nightmare**.

---
#### 2. What kinds of public transport are there where you live?
We've got **underground** lines, **overground** trains, **trams**, and a **dense** **bus network**. Taxis and **ride-hailing** apps **count** as **private** hire rather than **mass** transit, but they're part of how people **get around** when buses **divert** or **strike** action **disrupts** services.

---
#### 3. Do you prefer travelling by bus or by train?
**Generally** the train—it's **faster** for longer **distances**, you **avoid** **traffic jams**, and you can **read** or **reply** to emails without **motion sickness** **getting** to you. Buses are **handy** for **last-mile** trips where there's no **station** nearby, even if **timetables** are **less** **predictable** **at rush hour**.

---
#### 4. What are the advantages of public transport?
**Environmental** benefits **spring to mind**—**fewer** cars mean **lower** **emissions** if the system runs **efficiently**. It's **often** cheaper than **fuel**, **parking**, and **wear and tear** on your own vehicle. For visitors and **students** without a car, it's **simply** **essential**; it **keeps** cities **moving**.

---
#### 5. What problems are there with public transport in your city?
**Overcrowding** on **peak** services is the **obvious** one—sometimes you **squeeze** onto a **carriage** and **barely** have room to **breathe**. **Delays** from **signal** faults or **engineering** works **frustrate** everyone, and **fares** **creep up** year on year even when **reliability** doesn't **improve**.

---
#### 6. How do you think public transport could be improved?
I'd **prioritise** **frequent** **off-peak** services and **real-time** **displays** so people aren't **left guessing** at **platforms**. **Cleaner**, **accessible** vehicles and **step-free** access at more **stops** would help **elderly** passengers and parents with **pushchairs**. **Integrated** **ticketing** across buses and trains would **cut** **hassle** too.
