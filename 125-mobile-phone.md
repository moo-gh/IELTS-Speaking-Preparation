#### 1. Do you use a mobile phone often?
**Yes**, **constantly**—**messages**, **maps**, **banking**, **study** **apps**, and **photos** **all** **run** through it **daily**. I **try** to **put** it **down** during **meals** and **deep** **study**, but **honestly** it's **rarely** **far** from my **hand**.

---
#### 2. What do you use your mobile phone for?
**Messaging**, **calls**, **navigation**, **language** **practice**, **music**, **camera** **shots**, and **checking** **schedules**. **Social** **media** **eats** **time** **too**; I **use** **timers** when **scrolls** **threaten** to **swallow** **evenings**.

---
#### 3. When did you get your first mobile phone?
**Around** **secondary** **school**—**mainly** for **calling** **parents** after **classes**, not **smart** **features** like **today**. **Rules** were **strict** at **first**; **data** and **apps** **came** **later** as **needs** **grew**.

---
#### 4. Do you think mobile phones are important?
**Very**, **for** **modern** **life**—**safety**, **work**, **study**, and **staying** **connected** **depend** on them **now**. **Overuse** **hurts** **sleep** and **focus**, but **living** **without** one **feels** **impractical** in a **big** **city**.

---
#### 5. Would you like to change your mobile phone?
**When** **mine** **slows** **badly** or the **battery** **dies** **fast**—**not** **every** **year** like **some** **friends**. I **prefer** **mid**-**range** **models** that **last** **three** **years** over **chasing** **flagship** **launches**.

---
#### 6. Do you think children should have mobile phones?
**Older** **children**, **maybe**, with **limits**—**safety** and **family** **contact** **matter**, but **unlimited** **access** **damages** **attention** and **sleep**. **Parents** should **set** **rules** and **model** **healthy** **habits** **themselves**.
