#### 1. Do you like wearing hats?
**Sometimes**—**sun** **hats** and **warm** **beanies** **make** **sense** in **extreme** **weather**, but I **don't** **wear** them **daily** for **style**. **Caps** **feel** **casual**; **formal** **hats** **look** **great** on **others** but **odd** on **me** in **mirrors**.

---
#### 2. Do you wear hats often?
**Not** **really**—**maybe** on **sunny** **holidays**, **winter** **walks**, or **bad** **hair** **days** hidden under a **cap**. **Indoors** I **almost** **never** **keep** them **on** unless **tradition** **requires** it at a **specific** **event**.

---
#### 3. What kind of hats do you like?
**Simple** **baseball** **caps** and **plain** **knitted** **hats** without **loud** **logos**. **Wide**-**brim** **straw** **hats** for **beach** **trips**; **anything** **too** **stiff** or **tall** **feels** **costume**-**like** on **city** **streets**.

---
#### 4. Did you wear hats as a child?
**Yes**—**school** **trips** meant **sun** **hats** **parents** **labelled** with **names**, and **winters** brought **woolly** **hats** that **itched** my **forehead**. I **lost** **several** on **playgrounds** and **buses** **before** I **learned** to **check** **coat** **pockets**.

---
#### 5. Why do people wear hats?
**Protection** from **sun**, **cold**, and **rain**; **uniforms** for **jobs**; **fashion** and **cultural** **identity** **too**. **Some** **wear** them to **hide** **hair** **loss** or **bad** **cuts**—**practical** and **personal** **reasons** **mix** **together**.

---
#### 6. Are hats popular in your country?
**Seasonally** and **regionally**—**mountain** **areas** and **cold** **winters** **push** **warm** **hats**; **coastal** **summer** **brings** **caps** and **visors**. **Traditional** **headwear** **appears** at **weddings** and **ceremonies**, but **everyday** **fashion** **hats** are **less** **common** than in **some** **Western** **countries**.
