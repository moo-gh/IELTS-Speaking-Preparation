#### 1. What kind of food do you like?
I'm quite **partial to** Middle Eastern and Mediterranean dishes—things with **grilled** meat, fresh herbs, and **tangy** yoghurt sauces. I also enjoy a good **homemade** stew when the weather turns cold, because it feels **comforting** and **filling** without being too heavy.

---
#### 2. Do you like cooking? Why or why not?
I **do enjoy** cooking when I'm not **rushed**, because it's a **creative** outlet and I like knowing exactly what's gone into a meal. That said, after a long day at work, I sometimes **can't be bothered** and I'd rather **order in** or heat something simple.

---
#### 3. Do you prefer eating at home or eating out?
**It depends on the occasion**. For everyday meals, I prefer eating at home—it's usually **healthier**, cheaper, and more relaxed. For **catching up** with friends or celebrating something, eating out is **more fun** because of the **atmosphere** and not having to do the washing-up.

---
#### 4. Is food expensive where you live?
**Generally speaking**, yes—**eating out** in particular can **add up** quickly if you go to **mid-range** or **upmarket** places. **Groceries** have also become pricier, especially **fresh produce** and **imported** items, though you can still **keep costs down** if you cook **from scratch** and shop around.

---
#### 5. What kinds of food are popular in your country?
You'll find a lot of **rice-based** dishes, **kebabs**, and **hearty** soups, often **seasoned** with saffron, turmeric, or herbs. Street food is popular in cities—**quick**, **savoury** snacks people grab on their lunch break. Sweet pastries and **dried fruits** are also **staples** at gatherings and holidays.

---
#### 6. Have your eating habits changed since you were a child?
Definitely. As a child I was **picky** and ate **whatever** my parents put on the table. Now I'm more **adventurous**—I'll try new cuisines and I'm more **mindful** of **portion sizes** and **balanced** meals. I've also cut down on **fizzy drinks** and **processed** snacks compared to my teenage years.
