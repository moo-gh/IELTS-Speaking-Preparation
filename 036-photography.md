#### 1. Do you like taking photographs?
Yes—mostly **casual** shots on my phone when something **catches my eye**: good light on a building, a **street** scene, or friends at a **get-together**. I'm not **aiming for** **gallery** quality; I just like having **visual** reminders of a day out or a trip.

---
#### 2. Do you prefer to use a phone or a camera to take photos?
**These days** I **almost always** use my phone—it's **on hand**, the quality is **more than good enough** for social media, and I don't want to **lug around** a heavy **DSLR** everywhere. If I were **serious** about **landscape** work or **low-light** shots, a proper camera would **make sense**, but for everyday use the phone wins on **convenience**.

---
#### 3. What do you do with the photos you take?
Most of them **live in** my **camera roll** until I **sort** them out; the best ones get **backed up** to the cloud so I don't **lose** them if the phone **dies**. I **post** the **odd** picture online, and every year or so I **put together** a small **album** for family—nothing **fancy**, just something you can **flick through** on the sofa.

---
#### 4. Are there many photographs in your home?
There are a few **framed** prints in the **hallway** and on the **mantelpiece**—mostly **family** portraits and wedding photos from **relatives**. We haven't **covered** the walls with pictures; I think **less is more** so the place doesn't feel **cluttered**.

---
#### 5. Do you like looking at photographs of yourself?
**It depends on the context**. **Candid** shots from a night out can be funny, but I **cringe** at **stiffly posed** ones where I'm **clearly** **uncomfortable** in front of the lens. As I've got older I've become a bit more **at ease** with it—you learn to **not pick** **yourself** **apart** over every **tiny** **flaw**.

---
#### 6. Would you like to learn more about photography?
I'd **be open to** a short **workshop** on **composition** and **exposure**—the basics that would help me **get more out** of the phone camera without **geeking out** over **gear**. **Full-time** study or **professional** training isn't on my **radar**; it's a **sideline** interest, not a **career** plan.
