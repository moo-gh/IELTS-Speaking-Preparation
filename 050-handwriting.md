#### 1. Do you often write by hand?
**Less** than I **used** to—**most** of my **work** and **note-taking** is on a **laptop** or **phone** now, so **fountain** **pens** and **lined** **pads** are **mainly** for **journals**, **greeting** **cards**, and **filling** in **forms** that **refuse** to go **fully** **digital** on **principle**.

---
#### 2. Is handwriting still important, in your view?
**In** **some** **contexts**, **yes**—a **legible** **signature** and **tidy** **handwriting** on **exam** **answer** **sheets** or **official** **paperwork** can **still** **matter** for **clarity** and **trust**, even if we **rarely** **draft** **long** **texts** with a **pen** **any** **more**.

---
#### 3. How did you feel about handwriting when you were at school?
I **tolerated** it rather than **loved** it—**drills** in **cursive** **felt** a bit **laborious**, but I **took** **pride** in my **exercise** **book** not looking **scrawled** and **illegible**. **Teachers** **stressed** **neatness** for a **reason**: **examiners** **can't** **mark** what they **can’t** **read** **at** a **glance**.

---
#### 4. Do you prefer writing by hand or typing?
**Typing** is **faster** for me on **lengthy** **pieces**, with **editing** and **spelling** **tools** on **side**, and it **saves** my **wrist** on **heavier** **days**. I **handwrite** when I need to **sketch** a **plan**, **remember** a **line** more **vividly**, or **unplug** and **focus** without **notification** **noises** **buzzing** in.

---
#### 5. Have your handwriting skills changed as you've got older?
**They've**—**predictably**—**slipped** a **little** in **consistency** because I **rarely** **practice** **the** way I **did** in **class**, **though** the **shapes** of my **letters** are **broadly** the **same**. If I **slow** down, it **tightens** up; if I'm **rushing**, it can **morph** into a **hasty** **scrawl** I'd **struggle** to **decode** **weeks** **later** myself.

---
#### 6. Would you say your handwriting is neat?
**It's** **decent** when I'm **concentrating**—**even** **enough** for **envelopes** and **labelled** **folders** without **apologising**—but I wouldn't **enter** a **calligraphy** **competition**. **Line** **height** and **spacing** are **where** I **tend** to get **a** **bit** **messy** when the **list** in my **head** is **racing** **faster** than my **fingers** **can** **form** the **ink**.
