#### 1. Did your school have strict rules?
**Fairly** **strict**—**uniforms**, **bell** **times**, **phone** **bans** in **class**, and **clear** **punishments** for **cheating** or **fighting**. **Some** **rules** **felt** **petty**, like **hair** **length** **debates**, but **overall** they **kept** **chaos** **manageable** in **large** **classes**.

---
#### 2. Do you think students benefit from having rules?
**Yes**, **when** **rules** are **clear** and **fair**—they **teach** **discipline**, **respect**, and **how** **groups** **function** **without** **constant** **arguments**. **Too** **many** **micromanaging** **rules** **kill** **creativity**; **too** **few** **invite** **bullying** and **wasted** **lesson** **time**.

---
#### 3. Do you prefer more rules or fewer rules?
**Fewer** **but** **enforced** **consistently**—students **respect** **boundaries** they **understand**, not **endless** **lists** **nobody** **follows**. **Freedom** within **basic** **safety** and **honesty** **rules** **works** **better** than **treating** **teenagers** like **soldiers**.

---
#### 4. Were your teachers strict when you were young?
**Mixed**—**math** and **science** **teachers** **often** **ran** **tight** **ships**; **art** and **PE** **allowed** **more** **noise**. **Strict** **doesn't** **mean** **cruel**; the **best** **ones** **explained** **why** a **rule** **existed** instead of **just** **shouting**.

---
#### 5. Did your parents have strict rules for you?
**Moderately**—**curfews**, **homework** **before** **screens**, and **polite** **behaviour** toward **elders** were **non**-**negotiable**. **Friends** and **hobbies** had **flex** if **grades** **stayed** **decent**; **lying** or **disrespect** **triggered** **real** **consequences**.

---
#### 6. Are rules important in society?
**Essential**—**traffic** **lights**, **queue** **culture**, **tax** **honesty**, and **basic** **laws** **stop** **daily** **life** from **becoming** **survival** **of** the **loudest**. **People** **chafe** at **bad** **rules**, but **living** **without** **any** **structure** **costs** **more** than **following** **sensible** **ones**.
