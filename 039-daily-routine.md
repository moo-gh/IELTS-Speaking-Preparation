#### 1. Do you think it's important to have a daily routine for your study?
Yes—**some** structure **stops** me from **procrastinating** or **studying** **on a whim** when I'm already tired. A **consistent** **slot** helps me **build** **habits** and **track** progress. **That said**, the routine can't be **so rigid** that I **burn out**; **flexibility** matters too when work or life **gets in the way**.

---
#### 2. How do you organise your study time?
I **block out** **fixed** windows in my calendar—**usually** **short** **sessions** rather than one **marathon**—and I **prioritise** **weak** areas first when my **concentration** is **sharpest**. I also **keep** a simple **checklist** so I know what's **due** this week and I'm not **scrambling** the night before.

---
#### 3. What is your daily study routine?
On a **typical** weekday I'll **review** **vocabulary** over breakfast, **listen** to a podcast or **model** answers on the **commute**, then do **focused** practice—**timed** **speaking** or **writing**—after dinner for about an hour. At weekends I might **mix** **mock** **tasks** with **lighter** **revision** so it still feels **sustainable**.

---
#### 4. Do you ever change your plans?
**All the time**—if a **deadline** **pops up** at work or I'm **under the weather**, I **reschedule** instead of **forcing** myself through a **useless** **session**. I try to **swap** slots rather than **skip** studying **altogether**, so the week still **balances** out **overall**.

---
#### 5. Have you ever changed your routine?
Definitely. I used to **cram** late at night, but I **realised** I **wasn't retaining** much, so I **shifted** **bulk** study to the morning and **kept** evenings for **review** only. **Small tweaks** like that have made me **more productive** without **upping** the **hours**.

---
#### 6. What's your favourite time of the day?
I'm **most alert** in **mid-morning**—after coffee but before **post-lunch** **slump**—so that's when I **tackle** **trickier** **exercises**. **Golden hour** in **early** evening is nice too for **unwinding** and **walking**, which **clears my head** after **screen time**.

---
#### 7. Do you go to sleep early or stay up late?
**It depends on the week**, but I'm **trying** to **turn in** **reasonably** early during **intense** study periods—**consistent** sleep **boosts** memory. I still **stay up** **occasionally** if I'm **wired** after a stressful day, though I **pay** for it the next morning when the **alarm** **goes off**.
