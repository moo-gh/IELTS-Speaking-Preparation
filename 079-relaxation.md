#### 1. What do you do to relax?
**Walk** **without** **headphones** **sometimes**, **stretch**, **make** **tea**, or **watch** something **light** that **doesn't** **demand** **plot** **tracking**. **Reading** **fiction** **works** when I'm **not** **too** **tired**; **otherwise** I **accept** **early** **bed** as **the** **honest** **option**.

---
#### 2. Is relaxation important?
**Absolutely**—**nonstop** **stress** **blurs** **judgment**, **ruins** **sleep**, and **makes** **small** **problems** **feel** **catastrophic**. **Rest** isn't **lazy**; it's **maintenance** so **study** and **work** **don't** **collapse** **mid**-**week**.

---
#### 3. Do you relax more on weekdays or weekends?
**Weekends**, **clearly**—**longer** **blocks** for **friends**, **nature**, and **household** **tasks** **without** **alarm** **pressure**. **Weekdays** I **steal** **short** **breaks** between **sessions** rather than **expecting** **deep** **calm**.

---
#### 4. Where is your favourite place to relax?
**Home** **sofa** with **dim** **light**, or a **quiet** **park** **bench** when **weather** **cooperates**. **Busy** **cafés** **relax** me **socially** but **not** **mentally**; I **need** **either** **silence** or **trusted** **company**.

---
#### 5. Did you relax differently when you were young?
**Yes**—**play** **was** **default** **relaxation**: **football**, **bikes**, **comics**, **no** **guilt** about **doing** **nothing** **productive**. **Adult** **relaxation** **requires** **planning** and **fighting** **phone** **habits** I **didn't** **have** as a **kid**.

---
#### 6. Do you think people relax enough nowadays?
**Many** **don't**—**phones** **extend** **work** into **evenings**, and **comparison** **culture** **keeps** **people** **wired**. **Some** **over**-**relax** through **endless** **scrolling** that **isn't** **true** **rest**; **balance** between **activity** and **genuine** **downtime** **feels** **rare**.
