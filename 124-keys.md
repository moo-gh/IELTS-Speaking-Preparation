#### 1. Do you always bring a lot of keys with you?
**Not** **many**—**home** **key**, **maybe** a **locker** or **bike** **key**, and **that's** **enough** **daily**. **Huge** **keyrings** **jangle** **annoyingly** and **bulge** in **pockets**; I **keep** **extras** **at** **home** instead of **carrying** **everything**.

---
#### 2. Have you ever lost your keys?
**Once**, **briefly**—I **panicked** until I **found** them **inside** a **jacket** **pocket** I'd **already** **checked** **twice**. **Since** **then**, I **pat** my **bag** at **doorways** like a **small** **ritual** **before** **leaving**.

---
#### 3. Do you often forget your keys and lock yourself out?
**Rarely**, **thankfully**—I **hang** keys on a **hook** **near** the **door** the **moment** I **enter**, so **grabbing** them **becomes** **automatic**. **Stressful** **mornings** **raise** the **risk**, but **habit** **usually** **saves** me.

---
#### 4. Do you think it is a good idea to leave your keys with a neighbour?
**Only** if **trust** is **strong**—**close** **family** **neighbours** **maybe**, **strangers** **never**. **Spare** **keys** **help** in **emergencies**, but **security** **matters** **more** than **convenience** when **you** **don't** **know** **someone** **well**.

---
#### 5. Do you keep a spare key?
**Yes**, **one** **copy** with **parents** and **another** **hidden** **securely** at **home** for **worst**-**case** **lockouts**. **Replacing** **locks** **costs** **more** than **planning** **ahead** **properly**.

---
#### 6. Do you think electronic keys will replace traditional keys?
**Gradually**, **yes**—**keycards**, **codes**, and **phone** **apps** **spread** in **new** **buildings** because they're **convenient**. **Traditional** **keys** **still** **feel** **reliable** when **batteries** **die** or **systems** **glitch** **midnight**.
