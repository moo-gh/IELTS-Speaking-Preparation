#### 1. Do you enjoy watching films?
Yes—it's one of my **go-to** ways to **wind down** after work. I like being **drawn into** a story for a couple of hours, especially when the **soundtrack** and **cinematography** are strong. I don't watch **mindless** blockbusters every week, but a **well-made** drama or thriller **hits the spot**.

---
#### 2. What kind of films do you like best?
I'm **partial to** **character-driven** stories and **slow-burn** mysteries where you have to **piece things together**. I also enjoy **offbeat** comedy from time to time, as long as the humour doesn't feel **forced**. Pure **horror** isn't really my thing—I **scare** too easily.

---
#### 3. Do you prefer watching films at home or at the cinema?
**It depends**. For big **spectacle** releases—the kind that **benefit from** a huge screen and **surround sound**—I'd rather go to the cinema. For everything else, **streaming** at home is more **convenient**: you can pause, **grab a snack**, and you don't have to **trek** across town or pay **premium** prices for **popcorn**.

---
#### 4. How often do you watch films?
Probably one or two a week on average, but it **varies**. Some weeks I'm **swamped** and only **squeeze in** a short episode of something; other times I'll **binge** a **trilogy** at the weekend. I'm trying not to **replace** sleep with **late-night** screen time.

---
#### 5. Did you watch more films as a child than you do now?
I'd say I watched **family-friendly** films **more regularly** as a kid—**animated** features on **rainy** weekends and trips to the cinema with school. These days I have less **free time**, but I probably watch a **wider range** of genres and I'm more **picky** about what I **commit** to.

---
#### 6. Are foreign films popular in your country?
They're **gaining ground**, especially among **younger** viewers who read **subtitles** without **complaining**. **Hollywood** still **dominates** **mainstream** **billboards**, but **streaming platforms** have made Korean dramas, European art-house films, and Turkish series much more **accessible** than they used to be.
