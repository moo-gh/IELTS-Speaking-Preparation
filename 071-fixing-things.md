#### 1. Can you fix things?
**Basic** **stuff** **only**—**tightening** **screws**, **resetting** a **router**, **patching** **small** **holes** in **walls**, or **replacing** a **shower** **head**. **Electrics** beyond a **fuse** **box** and **anything** with **gas** I **leave** to **professionals**; I **know** my **limits** **before** I **cause** a **bigger** **bill**.

---
#### 2. Did anybody teach you to fix things when you were a child?
**My** **father** **showed** me how to **change** a **bike** **tyre** and **use** a **hammer** **without** **smashing** **thumbs**. **Grandparents** **fixed** **appliances** **longer** than **modern** **brands** **expect**; I **watched** more than I **practised**, but it **taught** me **patience** and **tool** **names**.

---
#### 3. Do you think people should learn to fix things?
**Yes**, **at** a **basic** **level**—it **saves** **money**, **reduces** **waste**, and **builds** **confidence** when **something** **minor** **breaks** at **home**. **Complex** **repairs** **need** **training**; **DIY** **heroes** **sometimes** **make** **problems** **worse** when **pride** **overrides** **sense**.

---
#### 4. Have you ever asked someone to teach you how to fix things?
**Once** a **friend** **walked** me through **bleeding** **radiators** and **unclogging** a **sink** with **simple** **tools**. **YouTube** **fills** the **gaps** **now**, but **live** **help** **matters** when you **don't** know which **valve** **turns** **what** **off**.

---
#### 5. What do you do when something breaks?
**Assess** whether it's **urgent** or **cosmetic**—**leaks** get **immediate** **action**, **wobbly** **chairs** wait for **weekends**. I **search** the **error** **code**, **try** a **safe** **fix**, then **call** a **technician** if **smoke**, **smell**, or **sparks** **appear**. **Renting** means I **notify** the **landlord** **too**.

---
#### 6. Is it common for people to repair things in your country?
**Older** **generations** **did** it **more**; **younger** **people** **often** **replace** **cheap** **goods** because **parts** are **hard** to **find** or **labour** **costs** **outweigh** a **new** **item**. **Repair** **cafés** and **right**-**to**-**repair** **talk** are **growing**, but **convenience** **still** **wins** for **many** **busy** **households**.
