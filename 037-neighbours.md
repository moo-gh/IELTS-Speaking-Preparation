#### 1. Do you know the people who live next door to you?
**To some extent**, yes—I know their **first names** and we've **exchanged** numbers in case of **emergencies**, but we're not **close friends**. In a **block of flats** you **cross paths** in the **lift** or **lobby** fairly often, so you **nod** hello even if you don't **stop for** a long chat.

---
#### 2. How often do you see or talk to your neighbours?
Probably a **handful** of times a month—**quick** **catch-ups** when we're both **taking out** the **recycling**, or if someone needs help **carrying** **groceries** **upstairs**. It's **friendly** but **low-key**; nobody **expects** you to **pop round** for coffee every weekend.

---
#### 3. What do you think makes a good neighbour?
**Respect** for **noise** tops the list—**thin walls** mean **loud** parties or **late-night** music **get on** people's nerves fast. **Reliability** matters too: **letting** each other know if a **parcel** **arrives** or if there's a **leak** in the **shared** hallway. You don't have to **socialise** constantly; you just need to be **reasonable** and **considerate**.

---
#### 4. Would you prefer to live in a place where neighbours know each other well, or where everyone keeps to themselves?
I'd **lean towards** a **middle ground**—enough **community spirit** that you feel **safe** and **looked out for**, but not so **intrusive** that you **can't** **switch off** at home. **Tight-knit** streets can be **wonderful** for families, yet I'm also **grateful** for **privacy** after a **hectic** day at work.

---
#### 5. Have you ever had any problems with neighbours?
Nothing **major**—**once** we had a **misunderstanding** about **parking** spaces, but we **sorted it out** **face-to-face** in five minutes. I've been lucky; I know friends who've **clashed** over **renovation** noise or **pets** **barking** at night, which can **drag on** if people won't **compromise**.

---
#### 6. Is it important for children to get to know their neighbours?
**Broadly speaking**, yes—it helps them **learn** **social skills** and **trust** that there are **adults nearby** if they need help. **That said**, parents still have to **teach** **boundaries** and **stranger-danger** basics so kids don't **wander** off with someone they barely know. **Balance** is everything.
