#### 1. Do you prefer typing or handwriting?
**Typing** for **long** **work**—**essays**, **emails**, and **notes** **flow** **faster** with **easy** **editing**. I **handwrite** for **quick** **diagrams**, **flashcards**, or when I **want** to **remember** something **without** **notification** **distractions**.

---
#### 2. Do you type on a desktop or laptop keyboard every day?
**Yes**, **almost** **every** **day** on a **laptop**—**classes**, **assignments**, **messages**, and **job** **applications** **all** **run** through **keys**. **Desktop** **keyboards** **feel** **better** for **long** **sessions**, but **portability** **wins** **most** **weeks**.

---
#### 3. When did you learn how to type on a keyboard?
**Around** **primary** **school** **computer** **classes**, then **properly** in **secondary** **school** when **essays** **moved** **online**. **Touch**-**typing** **never** became **perfect**—I **still** **glance** at **keys** on **tired** **nights**.

---
#### 4. How do you improve your typing?
**Daily** **use** **mainly**—**essays**, **chat**, and **occasional** **speed** **games** when I **feel** **motivated**. **Posture**, **wrist** **breaks**, and **keeping** **screens** at **eye** **level** **help** **more** than **chasing** **records** **alone**.

---
#### 5. Do you think typing is an important skill today?
**Essential**—**universities**, **offices**, and **even** **shop** **jobs** **expect** **basic** **keyboard** **fluency**. **Slow** **typing** **wastes** **hours** **over** a **semester** and **raises** **stress** before **deadlines**.

---
#### 6. What do you use typing for most often?
**Study** **notes**, **research** **summaries**, **emails**, **forms**, and **long** **messages** to **friends** when **voice** **notes** **feel** **too** **casual**. **Coding** and **spreadsheets** **appear** **sometimes** **too**, depending on **the** **course**.
