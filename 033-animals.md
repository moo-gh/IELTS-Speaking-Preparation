#### 1. Are you an animal lover?
I'd say yes, **for the most part**. I find animals **fascinating** to watch—birds, cats, even insects in the garden—and I respect the fact that they have their own **instincts** and **personalities**. I'm not someone who wants **exotic** pets at home, but I **appreciate** wildlife from a **respectful** distance.

---
#### 2. Did you have any pets when you were a child?
We had a **tabby** cat for years when I was growing up. She was quite **independent**—she'd **curl up** on the sofa in winter but **disappear** for hours when the weather was nice. Looking back, having a pet taught me **basic responsibility**, like keeping her **water bowl** full and not **teasing** her when she wanted peace.

---
#### 3. What animals are popular as pets where you live?
Cats and dogs are still the **most common** choices, especially in **apartment** buildings where a smaller dog or a quiet cat **fits** daily life. Some families keep **goldfish** or budgies because they're **low-maintenance**, and in rural areas you occasionally see people with **guard dogs** or **poultry** in the yard.

---
#### 4. Why do some people prefer not to keep pets?
Fair reasons, really—**allergies**, **landlord** rules, long work hours, or frequent travel make it **impractical**. Pets aren't cheap either: **vet bills**, food, and **grooming** **add up**. Some people simply **don't want** the **commitment** of caring for another living thing for fifteen years or more.

---
#### 5. Should wild animals be kept in zoos?
It's **not black and white**. Well-run zoos can support **breeding programmes** and **educate** the public, which **arguably** helps **conservation**. On the other hand, I'd hate to see intelligent animals in **tiny enclosures** with nothing to do. I think standards matter: **spacious** habitats and **ethical** care should be **non-negotiable**.

---
#### 6. What can people do to protect endangered species?
Individuals can **donate** to reputable **charities**, avoid products linked to **habitat destruction**, and **speak up** when laws are **watered down**. **Reducing waste** and **carbon footprints** indirectly protects ecosystems too. Governments and **enforcement** against **poaching** are just as important—**grassroots** action alone won't **fix** everything.
