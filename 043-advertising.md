#### 1. Are there many advertisements in your country?
**Absolutely**—they're **hard to** **escape**, from **billboards** on **commutes** to **pre-roll** **ads** before **videos** and **sponsored** **posts** in **social** **feeds**. **Outdoor** advertising feels **especially** **dense** in **city** **centres**, where **brands** **compete** for **attention** at **eye level**.

---
#### 2. Where do you often see advertisements?
**Most** **frequently** **online**—**search** results, **streaming** **platforms**, and **apps**—because that's where I **spend** **bulk** **screen** time. **Offline**, I **notice** them on **public transport**, in **lift** **panels**, and **sometimes** in **podcasts** as **read-out** **sponsor** **messages**.

---
#### 3. Do you pay attention to advertisements?
**Selectively**. If an ad is **relevant**—say, **language-learning** **tools** or **travel** **deals**—I'll **watch** it **properly**. **Random** **pop-ups** or **loud** **autoplay** clips **tend to** **annoy** me, so I **tune** them out or **skip** when I **can**.

---
#### 4. What kind of advertisement do you like?
I **respond** better to **clear**, **honest** **copy** and **clever** **humour** than to **hype** and **fake** **urgency**. **Short** **story-driven** **ads** can be **memorable** if they're **emotionally** **relatable**, not just **product** **specs** **shouted** at you.

---
#### 5. Is there an advertisement you remember well?
There was a **public** **health** **campaign** about **sleep** and **stress** that used **simple** **imagery** and a **calm** **voice-over**—**nothing** **flashy**, but it **stuck** because it felt **respectful** of the **audience**. **Contrast** that with **gimmicky** **ads** I **forget** **five seconds** **later**.

---
#### 6. Do you think advertising influences what people buy?
**To some extent**, yes—**repeated** **exposure** **shapes** **preferences** even when people **claim** they're **immune**. **Reviews** and **word of mouth** still **matter**, but **targeted** ads can **nudge** you at the **exact** moment you're **already** **considering** a **purchase**.

---
#### 7. Should advertising aimed at children be controlled?
**Broadly speaking**, yes—**young** kids **can't** **always** **tell** **persuasion** from **information**, especially with **toys** and **sugary** **snacks**. **Stricter** **rules** on **placement**, **claims**, and **data** **collection** seem **reasonable**, while still **allowing** **family-friendly** brands to **reach** **parents** **responsibly**.
