#### 1. How often do you take a break during the day?
**Every** **few** **hours** when **studying**—**short** **walks**, **tea**, or **five** **minutes** **away** from the **screen** **stop** **headaches** **building**. On **lighter** **days**, **breaks** **stretch** **longer**; during **exams**, they **shrink** but **never** **vanish** **completely**.

---
#### 2. What do you usually do when you rest?
**Stretch**, **drink** **water**, **listen** to a **song**, **chat** with **family**, or **step** **outside** for **fresh** **air**. **Scrolling** **social** **media** **sometimes** **passes** as a **break**, though it **rarely** **refreshes** me **properly**.

---
#### 3. Do you take naps?
**Occasionally**, **on** **weekends** or **after** **bad** **nights**—**twenty** **minutes** **helps**, **longer** **naps** **leave** me **groggy**. **Weekday** **naps** **risk** **ruining** **evening** **sleep**, so I **avoid** them **before** **deadlines**.

---
#### 4. How do you feel after taking a nap?
**Clearer** if it was **short** and **timed**; **worse** if I **overslept** and **woke** **confused** about the **hour**. **A** **quick** **wash** **face** and **water** **usually** **reset** me **faster** than **coffee** **alone**.

---
#### 5. Is it important to take breaks while working or studying?
**Essential**—**marathon** **sessions** **without** **pauses** **kill** **focus** and **quality** **drops** **sharply** after **ninety** **minutes**. **Planned** **breaks** **feel** **less** **guilty** than **accidental** **burnout** **later**.

---
#### 6. Where is your favourite place to take a break?
**Balcony**, **kitchen** **window**, or a **quiet** **corner** of a **park** **near** **campus**—**anywhere** with **air** and **minimal** **noise**. **Crowded** **food** **courts** **work** **socially** but **not** when I **need** **real** **rest**.
