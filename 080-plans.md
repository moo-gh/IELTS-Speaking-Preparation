#### 1. Do you like making plans?
**Yes**, **for** **important** **things**—**exams**, **travel**, **appointments**—because **vague** **intentions** **collapse** when **life** **gets** **busy**. I'm **less** **enthusiastic** about **over**-**planning** **social** **evenings**; **some** **flex** **keeps** **them** **fun**.

---
#### 2. What kind of plans do you make?
**Study** **timetables**, **weekly** **to**-**do** **lists**, **budget** **sketches**, and **rough** **itineraries** before **trips**. **Daily** **plans** are **simple**—**three** **priorities** **max**—so I **don't** **drown** in **checkboxes**.

---
#### 3. Do you prefer planning or being spontaneous?
**Planning** for **work** and **tests**; **spontaneity** for **low**-**stakes** **weekends** when **weather** and **mood** **matter** **more** than **schedules**. **Pure** **spontaneity** **sounds** **romantic** until **tickets** **sell** **out**.

---
#### 4. Are you good at sticking to plans?
**Moderately**—I **hit** **deadlines** when **stakes** are **high**, but **minor** **tasks** **slide** if **energy** **drops** or **distractions** **win**. I **build** **buffer** **time** **now** instead of **pretending** I'll **become** **superhuman** **overnight**.

---
#### 5. Did you make plans when you were younger?
**Parents** and **teachers** **planned** **most** **things** for me—**school**, **meals**, **holidays**. I **started** **planning** **revision** **myself** in **upper** **secondary** **school**, **badly** at **first**, then **less** **badly** after **a** **few** **painful** **all**-**nighters**.

---
#### 6. Do you use any tools to help you plan?
**Calendar** **app**, **notes** on my **phone**, and **occasionally** a **paper** **planner** during **intense** **exam** **months**. **Reminders** **save** me from **forgetting** **bills** and **mock** **tests**; **without** them I'd **live** in **permanent** **low**-**grade** **panic**.
