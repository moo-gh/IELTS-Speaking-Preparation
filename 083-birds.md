#### 1. How do you feel about birds?
**Mostly** **positive**—**morning** **songs** and **flocks** **over** **parks** **feel** **alive** without **demanding** **much** from me. **Pigeons** **crowding** **benches** for **crumbs** are **less** **charming**, but **overall** I **enjoy** **noticing** them on **walks**.

---
#### 2. What birds are common where you live?
**Pigeons**, **sparrows**, **crows**, and **seagulls** **near** **coasts** or **large** **parks**; **higher** **areas** **add** **eagles** or **hawks** if you're **lucky**. **Balcony** **planters** **attract** **small** **finches** when **seeds** **spill** from **feeders** **neighbours** **hang**.

---
#### 3. Do you like watching birds?
**Casually** **yes**—I **pause** for **unusual** **colours** or **clever** **nest** **building**, but I'm **not** **carrying** **binoculars** on **weekends**. **Documentaries** about **migration** **impress** me **more** than **standing** **still** for **hours** in **cold** **wind**.

---
#### 4. Have you ever kept a bird as a pet?
**No** **personally**—**relatives** **had** **canaries** and **budgies** in **cages** when I **visited**, and I **liked** the **chirping** but **felt** **sorry** if **space** was **tight**. **Cats** and **open** **windows** **make** **bird** **pets** **risky** in **many** **flats** **anyway**.

---
#### 5. Would you like to learn more about birds?
**A** **bit**—**names** of **common** **local** **species** and **how** **migration** **routes** **work** would **enrich** **walks**. I **don't** **plan** **expert**-**level** **identification**, but **recognising** **calls** **would** **beat** **calling** **everything** a **"small** **brown** **bird"**.

---
#### 6. Are there many birds near your home?
**Moderate** **numbers**—**trees** in **side** **streets** **host** **sparrows**, and **dawn** **can** be **noisy** in **spring**. **Heavy** **traffic** **zones** **have** **fewer** **species**, but **parks** **ten** **minutes** **away** **compensate** if I **make** the **effort**.
