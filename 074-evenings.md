#### 1. Do you like evenings?
**Yes**—**evenings** **feel** like **permission** to **slow** **down** after **deadlines** and **commutes**. **Soft** **light**, **quieter** **streets**, and **no** **immediate** **alarm** **pressure** make them **easier** to **enjoy** than **hectic** **mornings**.

---
#### 2. What do you usually do in the evening?
**Cook** or **heat** **leftovers**, **review** **study** **notes** for **an** **hour**, then **watch** something **light** or **call** **family**. **Weeknights** I **avoid** **starting** **heavy** **tasks** **late**; **Fridays** I **stretch** the **evening** **without** **guilt**.

---
#### 3. Do you prefer mornings or evenings?
**Evenings**, **honestly**—I'm **more** **awake** **mentally** once **basic** **errands** are **done**. **Mornings** **work** when I **must**, but **creativity** and **conversation** **flow** **better** for me **after** **sunset**.

---
#### 4. What did you do in the evenings when you were young?
**Homework** **first**, then **TV**, **board** **games** with **siblings**, or **football** in the **street** until **parents** **called** us **in**. **Summer** **evenings** **lasted** **forever**; **winter** meant **earlier** **baths** and **bedtime** **rules**.

---
#### 5. Is your neighbourhood quiet in the evening?
**Mostly**—**residential** **streets** **settle** after **rush** **hour**, though **weekend** **noise** from **cafés** or **nearby** **main** **roads** **still** **filters** through. **Occasional** **parties** **break** the **calm**, but **nothing** **constant**.

---
#### 6. Would you like to change your evening routine?
**Slightly**—I'd **like** **less** **screen** **time** **right** before **sleep** and **more** **walking** after **dinner** when **weather** **allows**. **Consistency** **matters** **more** than **adding** **ten** **new** **habits** **at** **once**.
