#### 1. Do you often use maps?
**Yes**, **mostly** on my **phone** when I'm **new** to an **area** or **meeting** someone at a **vague** **landmark** like **"near** the **old** **bridge"**. **Familiar** **routes** I **memorise**, but **detours** from **roadworks** **send** me **back** to **maps** **fast**.

---

**Electronic** for **live** **traffic** and **step**-**by**-**step** **walking** **directions**; **paper** **only** when **signal** **dies** on **hikes** or in **museums** that **hand** out **floor** **plans**. **Screens** **rotate** with me; **folded** **atlases** **feel** **nostalgic** but **bulky** in a **bag**.

**A** **little** in **geography**—**scale**, **legend**, and **compass** **directions** on **printed** **sheets**. **Real** **skill** came **later** with **GPS** **apps**; **school** **taught** **concepts**, not **the** **tap**-**to**-**navigate** **habits** I **use** **now**.

#### 5. Do you think it is important to be able to read maps?
**Yes**, **because** **batteries** **die** and **apps** **glitch**—**basic** **orientation** **stops** **panic** when you're **offline**. **Relying** **only** on **voice** **prompts** **weakens** your **mental** **picture** of **how** **neighbourhoods** **connect**, **in** my **view**.

---
