#### 1. What environmental problems are most serious in your country?
I'd say **air quality** in big cities is a constant worry, especially **traffic emissions** and **industrial pollution**. Water scarcity is also becoming more **pressing** in some regions, and **waste management** can be an issue where **landfills** fill up faster than recycling **keeps pace**.

---
#### 2. What can individuals do to help protect the environment?
Small habits add up: using **reusable** bags and bottles, **cutting down on** single-use plastic, and choosing **public transport** or cycling when it's practical. People can also **reduce food waste** and buy fewer things they don't really need, because consumption often drives **carbon footprints**.

---
#### 3. Do you think governments or individuals should take more responsibility?
I think you need both. Governments can set **regulations**, invest in **renewable energy**, and improve **infrastructure**, but individuals still choose how they travel, shop, and heat their homes. If policy is strict but public attitude doesn't shift, progress can be **slow**—and the opposite is true too.

---
#### 4. Is climate change mainly caused by human activity?
The scientific **consensus** is that human activity has **accelerated** climate change through **greenhouse gas emissions**, deforestation, and heavy fossil fuel use. Natural factors exist, but the speed and scale of recent warming are hard to explain without **human impact**.

---
#### 5. Should companies be forced to become more environmentally friendly?
I believe **incentives** help, but **mandatory standards** matter for big polluters, otherwise some firms may **prioritise** short-term profit. Rules on emissions, packaging, and **transparent reporting** can **level the playing field** so responsible companies aren't **undercut** by those cutting corners.

---
#### 6. Do young people care more about the environment than older generations?
**Stereotypically**, yes—many young people grew up hearing about climate change and join **campaigns** or **protests**. That said, I've met older relatives who were **frugal** and eco-minded long before it was trendy. I'd frame it as a mix of **awareness**, upbringing, and whether people feel they can afford "greener" choices.
